Preparation

Creating a first B2S backglass ›› A B2S backglass from scratch ››

There are a few things you have to do before starting the new B2S backglass project.


  • Look for a matching image
    Not every image is leading to a great backglass. If you found one and you're not happy with it don't hesitate to search once again. It will take 30 minutes or longer but these 30 minutes are a good invest when you're able to find a better backglass.


  • Maybe revise the image
    If necessary use Paint.NET, Gimp or Photoshop or other painting progs to 'pimp' the image. Especially Paint.NET is easy to use. I.e. there are some original LEDs in the image. No problem, some black color, a painting rectangle and a brush in Paint.NET are solving this problem.


  • Image resolution as high as possible
    A high resolution of the source backglass image is perfect. During the design process in the backglass designer you should reduce this 'monster' resolution. But for a pretty cool start it's great to have an image with i.e. 3500x2000 pixels.


  • Watch videos of the original pin
    There are a lot videos in the web showing the original pins without but also with some backglass viewing. Invest some time to find cool videos showing the illumination and the 'action' of the backglass.
    Here is a great video of 'Cosmic Gunfight': http://pinball.org/videos/gameplay-videos/cosmic-gunfight/


  • Get infos from WWW.IPDB.ORG
    Pretty sure you know this web address. To get more detailed infos about the pin this is the first address. If available download the manual of the cab. Normally you can find a lot great infos about the lamps, solenoids or the general illumination. Your first look should go to the lamp matrix and the solenoid matrix.


  • Do the job of an detective
    Sometimes it is not too easy to get the necessary infos. Don't give up, what you need for this detective's job is some experience: Experience where to find good backglass stuff, experience how to read the original manuals, experience with the designer. But I promise you, design your first backglass, play it for the first time on your cab and you're having great fun.



For our 'The Bally Game Show' I found some resources: A backglass image from the UVP backglass, a 'Behind backglass' image from ipdb.org and the original 'Operations manual' from Bally at ipdb.org too.

The resolution of the backglass image is a bit low, but right now this is the only image I found.

This is your first job: Download or get these resources too. Get the backglass image and check 'ipdb.org'.
